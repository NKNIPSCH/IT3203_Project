The Debt Avalanche Method

What is the Debt Avalanche Method?

The debt avalanche method is a debt repayment strategy that focuses on paying off debts with the highest interest rates first. This method is mathematically optimal, saving you the most money in interest charges over time.

The Process

  • List debts by highest to lowest interest rate
  • Make minimum payments on everything
  • Put extra money toward highest-interest debt
  • Once paid, move to next highest interest rate

Example Debts

  • Credit Card A: 22.9% APR
  • Credit Card B: 19.9% APR
  • Personal Loan: 12.5% APR
  • Car Loan: 5.9% APR

Key Benefits

  • Saves the most in interest charges
  • Mathematically optimal approach
  • Reduces total debt faster
  • Clear payoff timeline
  • Best for interest cost reduction

Considerations

Advantages

  • Maximum interest savings
  • Shorter total payoff time
  • Systematic approach
  • Mathematically efficient

Potential Drawbacks

  • Takes longer to see first payoff
  • Less early motivation
  • Requires more discipline

Is It Right For You?

The avalanche method might be your best choice if:

  • You want to pay the least interest possible
  • You have high-interest debt
  • You prefer mathematical optimization
  • You can stay motivated without quick wins

Remember: While the avalanche method saves the most money, choose the strategy that you'll stick with consistently.
