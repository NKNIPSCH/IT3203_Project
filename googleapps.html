Replace Google Apps

Google apps are convenient but come with privacy concerns. Here are some privacy-respecting alternatives.

Email Alternatives

Google's Gmail tracks and analyzes emails for advertising purposes. Consider switching to these secure providers:

  • ProtonMail: End-to-end encrypted, no tracking, based in Switzerland.
  • Tutanota: Fully encrypted, includes an encrypted calendar.
  • Zoho Mail: Business-focused, privacy-conscious alternative.

Cloud Storage Alternatives

Google Drive provides free cloud storage but scans files for content. Try these alternatives:

  • Nextcloud: Self-hosted cloud storage with complete control over your data.
  • Sync.com: End-to-end encryption, 5GB free.
  • pCloud: Secure storage with lifetime purchase options.

Office Suite Alternatives

Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides store all edits and can analyze content. These alternatives offer privacy:

  • LibreOffice: Free, open-source, offline office suite.
  • ONLYOFFICE: Supports collaborative work with strong privacy controls.
  • CryptPad: Fully encrypted, real-time collaboration.

Navigation and Maps Alternatives

Google Maps tracks your location history. Here are privacy-focused map services:

  • OpenStreetMap: Community-driven, open-source maps.
  • Organic Maps: Offline maps with no tracking.
  • Maps.me: Privacy-focused, works offline.

Steps to Transition Away from Google Apps

Making the switch from Google services can be done gradually:

  1. Step 1: Export your data using Google Takeout.
  2. Step 2: Start using a privacy-focused email provider like ProtonMail.
  3. Step 3: Move cloud files to Nextcloud or Sync.com.
  4. Step 4: Switch to an alternative document editor like LibreOffice or ONLYOFFICE.
  5. Step 5: Replace Google Maps with OpenStreetMap or Organic Maps.
