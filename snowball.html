The Debt Snowball Method

What is the Debt Snowball Method?

The debt snowball method, popularized by financial advisor Dave Ramsey, is a debt repayment strategy that focuses on paying off your smallest debts first, regardless of interest rates. Like a snowball rolling downhill, you build momentum as you pay off each debt, helping you stay motivated throughout your debt-free journey.

The Process

  1. List all debts from smallest to largest balance
  2. Make minimum payments on all debts
  3. Put extra money toward smallest debt
  4. After paying off smallest debt, apply that payment to next smallest
  5. Continue until all debts are eliminated

Example Scenario

Starting Debts:

  • Credit Card A: $500 (minimum: $25)
  • Personal Loan: $2,500 (minimum: $75)
  • Credit Card B: $4,000 (minimum: $100)
  • Car Loan: $10,000 (minimum: $200)

Payment Strategy:

  1. Make minimum payments: $400 total monthly
  2. Extra money goes to Credit Card A
  3. After Credit Card A, add $25 to Personal Loan
  4. Continue cascading payments upward

Key Benefits

  • Quick wins for motivation
  • Simple to understand and follow
  • Early visible progress
  • Builds positive financial habits
  • Perfect for small-win motivation

Considerations

Advantages

  • Psychological momentum
  • Clear, simple strategy
  • Early success reinforcement
  • Easy to track progress

Potential Drawbacks

  • Higher interest costs possible
  • Delayed high-interest debt payoff
  • Not mathematically optimal

Is It Right For You?

The snowball method might be your best choice if:

  • You value quick, visible results
  • You have multiple small debts
  • Past debt plans haven't worked
  • You prefer simple systems

Remember: The best strategy is one you'll maintain consistently. While the snowball method may cost more in interest than the avalanche method, its psychological benefits often outweigh the mathematical disadvantages.
