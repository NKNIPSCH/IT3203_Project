The Debt Snowball Method

What is the Debt Snowball Method?

The debt snowball method is a debt repayment strategy that focuses on paying off your smallest debts first, regardless of interest rates. Like a snowball rolling downhill, you build momentum as you pay off each debt, helping you stay motivated throughout your debt-free journey.

The Process

  • List debts from smallest to largest
  • Make minimum payments on everything
  • Put extra money toward smallest debt
  • Once paid, move to next smallest

Example Debts

  • Credit Card A: $500
  • Personal Loan: $2,500
  • Credit Card B: $4,000
  • Car Loan: $10,000

Key Benefits

  • Quick wins for motivation
  • Simple to understand and follow
  • Early visible progress
  • Builds positive financial habits
  • Perfect for small-win motivation

Considerations

Advantages

  • Psychological momentum
  • Clear, simple strategy
  • Early success reinforcement
  • Easy to track progress

Potential Drawbacks

  • Higher interest costs possible
  • Delayed high-interest debt payoff
  • Not mathematically optimal

Is It Right For You?

The snowball method might be your best choice if:

  • You value quick, visible results
  • You have multiple small debts
  • Past debt plans haven't worked
  • You prefer simple systems

Remember: The best strategy is one you'll maintain consistently. While the snowball method may cost more in interest than the avalanche method, its psychological benefits often outweigh the mathematical disadvantages.
